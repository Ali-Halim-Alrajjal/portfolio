Full-Scale Email and Domain Migration to AWS

At Yogous

I led the end-to-end migration of Yogous's digital infrastructure from GoDaddy to Amazon Web Services (AWS). The primary business drivers were to reduce operational costs, escape the limitations of a shared hosting environment, and gain the flexibility of a cloud-native ecosystem.

The project involved two parallel streams: email and domain management. For email, I orchestrated the seamless transition to Amazon WorkMail, where I provisioned and managed user mailboxes and created functional email groups to improve internal communication. Understanding the criticality of email data, I implemented a risk-mitigation strategy that included instructing all staff to create local backups of their mailboxes prior to the cutover.

For the domain migration, I meticulously documented and backed up all existing DNS records (including A, CNAME, and MX records) from GoDaddy to prevent service disruption. After transferring the domains to AWS Route 53, I reconfigured the records, which granted us granular control over our web presence. This move to Route 53 not only enhanced DNS management but also streamlined the integration of other AWS services and future third-party tools, significantly improving our architectural agility.
